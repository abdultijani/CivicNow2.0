Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers about how CivicNow works, how your representatives are identified, and how your information is handled.

About CivicNow

What is CivicNow?

CivicNow helps Massachusetts residents quickly find their elected representatives at the local, state, and federal levels.

Who is CivicNow for?

CivicNow is designed for Massachusetts residents who want an easier way to understand their representation and participate in civic life.

Is CivicNow affiliated with the government?

No. CivicNow is an informational civic engagement platform and is not a government agency or affiliated with any political party. For authoritative election information, always refer to the Massachusetts Secretary of State's Office .

What makes CivicNow different?

CivicNow focuses specifically on helping Massachusetts residents quickly identify their elected representatives and learn how to engage with them. By simplifying representative lookup and presenting contact information in one place, CivicNow aims to make civic engagement easier and more accessible for residents.

Using the Platform

How does CivicNow find my representatives?

When you enter your address, CivicNow identifies the government districts associated with that location and matches them with the officials who represent those districts.

What representatives will I see?

You may see representatives from several levels of government:

  • U.S. House of Representatives
  • U.S. Senators
  • Massachusetts State Representatives
  • Massachusetts State Senators
  • Local officials when available
Can I use CivicNow outside Massachusetts?

The current version of CivicNow focuses on Massachusetts residents. Future versions may expand to other states.

About the Information

What do I do if I see information that is outdated or wrong?

Please email us with a brief description of the issue and, if possible, a link to a reliable source. We take accuracy seriously and review all submissions promptly.

Does this website tell me who to vote for?

Absolutely not. CivicNow is strictly nonpartisan. We present factual, sourced information and let you draw your own conclusions. We do not rank candidates, assign scores, or make voting recommendations.

Why hasn't my city been added yet?

We're a small team expanding coverage across Massachusetts incrementally. If your area isn't listed yet, we're likely working on it. You can help by letting us know.

Where does your candidate and offical information come from?

We source our information from publicly available records including official state and municipal websites, candidate campaign sites, public financial disclosures, and published news coverage. We also reach out to the City Halls themselves for information they could provide. Where possible, we link directly to primary sources so you can verify everything yourself.

How often is the information updated?

We aim to review and refresh candidate profiles on a rolling basis, with more frequent updates during active election seasons. Information can occasionally lag behind real-world changes, but we'll always note the last-updated date on each profile.

How do you decide what information to include about a candidate?

We apply the same criteria to every candidate: office sought, current or prior roles, publicly stated policy positions, and publicly available biographical background. We do not include opinion, speculation, or unverified claims; regardless of political affiliation.

Can a candidate request to update or correct their information?

Yes. Candidates or their authorized representatives may contact us (link to contact form) to flag factual inaccuracies. We'll review the request and, if supported by verifiable sources, update the profile. We do not accept requests to remove accurate public information.

Questions on Voting

How do I register to vote in Massachusetts?

You can register online, by mail, or in person at your local town or city clerk's office. The deadline is 10 days before an election. Visit the Massachusetts Online Voter Registration page for step-by-step instructions and to check your registration status.

Where do I find my voting location?

Your voting location is assigned based on your registered address. You can find it using the Massachusetts Find My Election Information tool.

When is the next election?

Election dates vary by municipality. For a full schedule of upcoming elections, visit the Massachusetts Elections Division website. CivicNow may also highlight major upcoming elections in the future.

Privacy & Data

Is my address stored?

No. CivicNow does not permanently store addresses entered into the search tool.

Do I need to create an account to use this site?

No account is required. All candidate and election information on CivicNow is freely accessible without signing up or logging in.

Do you collect any of my personal data or information?

We do not collect names, email addresses, or any personally identifiable information from visitors browsing the site. If you contact us via email, we only use that information to respond to your message.
